Act II · The Framework
07 / 20
The distinction

Two kinds of uncertainty

Type 1
Aleatory
  • The randomness you manage by shuffling the deck
  • Variation within known options
  • AI's temperature, top-k, and top-p are aleatory tools
  • Creates variation within what the model already "knows"
Type 2
Epistemic
  • The unknowns you reduce by investigating and learning
  • Discovering what you don't know yet
  • Exploratory testing is fundamentally epistemic
  • Requires observing the real world

What goes where

01 · Human
Strategy & Risk
Identify what matters. Define the attack surface. Understand the business logic.
02 · Human
Exploration
Discover edge cases. Notice what's broken. Find what the spec didn't predict.
03 · AI

Generate boilerplate. Create regression suites. Expand known test patterns.

05 · Human
Curation
Review AI output. Delete the junk. Keep what matters.
06 · Human
Judgment
Decide if it ships. Own the outcome. Learn for next time.

Act IV · Tool 1
17 / 20
Tool 01
The Aleatory/Epistemic Test
Before delegating any task to AI, ask: "Am I asking AI to shuffle known options, or discover unknown ones?"

If it's shuffling → delegate.
If it's discovering → keep it human.

Use this to make every delegation decision in 10 seconds.

18 / 20
Tool 02
The Janitor Metric
Track how much time your team spends cleaning up AI output vs. doing actual work.

If janitor time exceeds 30% of total time, you have a category error. Reallocate the work.

Use this to measure whether AI is actually helping.
